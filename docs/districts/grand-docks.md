# The Grand Docks

## Gateway of Ayerstone

*The last light before the dark.*

The Grand Docks dominate the entire southwestern face of Ayerstone's central island. They are not a simple harbor. They are a cliffside city of platforms, cranes, cargo lifts, rope bridges, warehouses, taverns, shrines, customs houses, merchant booths, repair yards, and Arkflight berths.

The docks stretch from the upper face of Ayerstone down toward the lower broken ridges, stopping within roughly half a mile of the island's southern tips. From a distance they look like a second city bolted to the cliff.

At full capacity, the Grand Docks can berth roughly **300 Arkflight ships at once** and may handle around **700 ships in a busy day**, counting arrivals, departures, cargo transfers, short berths, repairs, and inspection traffic.

## First Impression

The Grand Docks are one of the great sights of Ayerstone.

Ships drift in from the Void with glowing Arkengines, torn sails, bright banners, brass hull fittings, cargo lanterns, and crews shouting across the dark. Levstone elevators rise and fall between terraces. Cranes swing cargo over open space. Dockworkers run across chain bridges and timber platforms. Merchants shout from rented booths. Priests bless ships. Inspectors argue with captains. Pickpockets work the stairways. Somewhere below, a tavern song is always being sung badly.

## Layout

### Lower Docks

The Lower Docks are the cheapest and roughest berths. Independent captains, damaged ships, desperate haulers, adventurers, smugglers, salvage crews, and crews who cannot afford better placement end up here.

The platforms are close to the underside cliffs and often half-shadowed by the island above. They are loud, cramped, wet with mist, and patched constantly. Rope bridges, old planks, crude cargo nets, cheap food stalls, and temporary repair tents are everywhere.

This is where sailors find last-minute work, questionable guides, cheap rooms, illegal cargo, and someone willing to ask fewer questions.

### Middle Docks

The Middle Docks are the busiest working levels.

Cargo moves constantly between warehouses, cranes, ropewrights, chandlers, customs offices, inspection yards, repair crews, merchant booths, and levstone lifts. Most legitimate cargo and passenger traffic passes through these tiers.

Travelers are most likely to see guild clerks, dock guards, porters, warehouse factors, contract scribes, food carts, animal handlers, Arkengine apprentices, and sailors between voyages.

### Upper Docks

The Upper Docks are prestigious white-marble terraces near the top of the southwest cliffs. Famous captains, noble vessels, diplomatic ships, major merchant craft, royal envoys, and renowned explorers dock here.

The stone is pale, polished, and enchanted against soot and salt. The cranes are larger, safer, and more elegant. The guard presence is heavier. The prices are obscene.

A captain who docks here is making a statement.

## Movement Between Levels

The docks are connected by:

- Wide cargo stairs
- Narrow sailor stairs
- Ramps for carts and beasts
- Rope bridges
- Chain bridges
- Crude pulley baskets
- Rudimentary lifts
- Levstone elevators
- Guild-controlled cargo platforms

The levstone elevators are one of the wonders of the docks, especially for first-time visitors. They rise smoothly through mist, rope traffic, shouting workers, and the glow of ship lanterns. They are also expensive, often delayed, and occasionally terrifying.

## Major Locations

### The Last Lantern

The Last Lantern is the most famous sailor's tavern in the Lower Docks.

Its sign shows a lantern hanging over a black horizon. Sailors say it is the last warm light before the Void takes you.

The tavern is crowded with deckhands, pilots, dock toughs, failed adventurers, smugglers, fortune seekers, and old captains who claim they are retired. The food is plain, the drinks are strong, and half the rumors in Ayerstone pass through its common room eventually.

Motto:

> The last light before the dark.

### Celestial Trade and Shipping Consortium

The Celestial Trade and Shipping Consortium is the most impressive building on the Upper Docks: a massive white-marble trade hall controlled by Lady Celestia Starweaver and House Starweaver interests.

It functions as:

- Shipping office
- Route authority
- Guild hall
- Contract house
- Noble receiving hall
- Merchant booth registry
- High-value cargo office
- Diplomatic trade chamber

Merchants can rent booths here to sell elite goods without sending them across the city to Merchant's Reach. The fees are brutal, but the buyers are rich.

### Twin Sentinels of the Horizon

Two colossal statues of Aeloria, Lady of the Endless Horizon, stand over the Upper Docks.

One is called **The Lady Who Welcomes**, facing returning ships.

The other is called **The Lady Who Calls**, facing the open Void.

Sailors pray to both. The first for safe return. The second for courage to depart.

### Guild of Arkengineers

Officially called the Guild of Arkengineers, but almost everyone calls them the Arkflight Mechanics.

Their hall sits on the upper-middle docks, close enough to wealthy captains to be respected and close enough to working crews to stay useful. The building is marked by brass gearwork, exposed engine pieces, glowing test chambers, reinforced stone walls, steam vents, and apprentices running in every direction.

They handle:

- Arkengine inspection
- Emergency repairs
- Engine certifications
- Apprentice training
- Fueling standards
- Component disputes
- Catastrophic failure investigations

No wise captain angers them.

### Dockmaster Courts

A maze of offices, open counters, waiting yards, and argument pits where captains negotiate berth assignments, cargo timing, damage claims, inspection delays, and fines.

Every captain hates the Dockmaster Courts.

Every captain needs them.

### Customs and Weigh Houses

Cargo is inspected, weighed, taxed, sealed, delayed, lost, found, and occasionally stolen here.

The clerks claim the system is efficient.

No one else agrees.

### Shrine Stairs

A steep stairway lined with small shrines to Aeloria and other travel gods. Sailors leave coins, knots of rope, carved ship tokens, broken compass needles, old sailcloth, and bottles of first-voyage liquor.

## Dock Economy

The docks are a city of temporary opportunity.

Common work includes:

- Cargo hauling
- Hull scraping
- Ropework
- Sail patching
- Guard duty
- Passenger escort
- Beast handling
- Food vending
- Message running
- Guide work
- Salvage crew hiring
- Warehouse labor
- Arkengine maintenance

A person can arrive with nothing and find work in an hour.

They can also disappear in less time.

## Law and Order

The Irystone Watch patrols the main routes and attempts to keep the peace, but dock law is practical, hurried, and uneven.

The Upper Docks receive swift protection.

The Middle Docks receive bureaucracy.

The Lower Docks receive whoever is available.

The Violet Guard rarely appears openly in the docks, but when they do, every tavern goes quiet.

## Crime and Smuggling

Smuggling exists at every dock level.

The lower tiers move illicit goods, undocumented passengers, stolen cargo, forged travel papers, black-market components, and strange relics from the Void.

The middle tiers hide crime behind paperwork.

The upper tiers call it private trade.

Rumors claim the Shadow Brotherhood has influence from the Lower Docks into the middle tiers, though it has not secured true control of the Upper Docks.

## Famous Dock Tale

One of the most repeated stories in the Grand Docks concerns a gnome accountant caught embezzling from Celestial Trade and Shipping.

According to dockside legend, Lady Celestia Starweaver herself dragged the gnome to the center of the docks before merchants, sailors, travelers, and captains. She lifted him into the air and threw him into the Void.

Some say the story is exaggerated.

Nobody embezzles from Celestial Trade twice.

## House Influence

- **House Starweaver** dominates elite shipping, route contracts, and the Consortium.
- **House Stormglass** is tied to shipbuilding, refits, experimental hull work, and repair contracts.
- **House Blackwake** maintains convoy, escort, and privateer offices.
- **House Ironmantle** controls ore lifts, stone freight, heavy cranes, and Deepstone traffic.
- **House Veyr** insures ships, cargo, captains, and sometimes terrible decisions.
- **House Marruk** sells guards, escorts, and dock security contracts.

## Adventure Hooks

- A ship arrives with no crew, its Arkengine still warm.
- A levstone elevator stops between levels with something alive in the cargo.
- A stolen crate from the Upper Docks appears in a Lower Docks tavern.
- A famous captain vanishes after visiting the Shrine Stairs.
- The Arkflight Mechanics quietly hire adventurers to investigate a sabotaged engine.
- The Shadow Brotherhood moves a relic through the Middle Docks under false customs seals.
- A priest of Aeloria claims one of the dock statues moved during the night.

## Dockside Sayings

> If it sails the Void, it eventually passes through here.

> The Upper Docks sell reputation. The Lower Docks sell survival.

> Never trust cargo that hums.
