# Crownward

## The Crown Above the Falls

*The Stone Endures.*

Crownward sits at the north-center height of Ayerstone, above Noble Row and overlooking Portal Lake. It is the seat of royal authority, home of House Aurelian, the royal courts, government ministries, ceremonial gardens, old fortifications, elite guards, and institutions that remind every visitor that Ayerstone is still a kingdom.

Because the city rises from south to north, Crownward feels physically above everything else.

## Appearance

Crownward is formal, old, expensive, and watched.

Its roads are pale stone and white marble. Violet banners hang from towers. Gold trim marks official buildings. Gardens are clipped and ceremonial. Fountains are polished. Statues of monarchs, explorers, martyrs, and house founders stand in plazas and along avenues.

The district is not quiet because it is empty. It is quiet because everyone knows when to lower their voice.

## Major Locations

### Crownward Palace

The seat of House Aurelian and the royal government.

The palace is built in white stone, gold trim, violet banners, long balconies, ceremonial courts, guarded arches, and gardens that overlook Portal Lake. Its highest terraces face the Waterplane Portal.

### The Hall of Laws

A massive court complex where royal decrees, noble disputes, trade-law cases, inheritance claims, city ordinances, house petitions, and major civic cases are heard.

The Hall of Laws is famous for its carved stone galleries, echoing chambers, and old legal archives.

### The Concordant Spire

The largest of Ayerstone's three mage guilds and schools, and the home of the **Fourfold Conclave**.

The blackstone spire rises 170 feet at the edge of Crownward. It has an 80-foot circular base, a sharp pentagonal crown, orbiting tradition towers, rune-chains, magical crystals, gargoyles, flight platforms, teleportation circles, and an entrance known as **the Gate That Is Not A Door**.

The four orbiting towers represent Arcane, Divine, Occult, and Primal traditions.

### The Concordant Plaza

A plaza near the Fourfold Conclave used for public magical demonstrations, controlled access to flight platforms, festival rites, and carefully supervised student events.

### The Hall of Petitions

The public petition building where citizens, guilds, houses, and foreign representatives submit formal requests to the Crown.

The line outside is often longer than the patience of those standing in it.

### The Chamber of Charts

A navigation archive and strategic records office containing star charts, old routes, chainbound surveys, drift records, and official copies of major route agreements.

### The Astrarium

A star and void observatory used by scholars, navigators, astrologers, and court seers.

### Lantern Walk

A memorial avenue lined with lanterns and statues honoring explorers, sailors, soldiers, and citizens lost beyond Ayerstone.

### Hall of Chains

A museum and civic archive dedicated to Ayerstone's founding, the great chains, the Chainbound Isles, and the engineering works that bind the city together.

### Horizon's Rest

A memorial park for sailors, explorers, Arkflight crews, and travelers who never returned.

## Law and Power

### Irystone Watch

The ordinary city watch, led by **Commander Tobias Finchwillow**, a satyr known for his monocle, polished uniform, rapier, cane, refined tastes, and sharp investigative mind.

The Watch handles ordinary law: patrols, theft, public order, market disputes, missing persons, dock incidents, and tavern trouble.

### Violet Guard

The Crown's elite investigative service, led by **Lord Commander Garrick Thornwall**, a barrel-chested human with a broad smile, five o'clock shadow, practical armor, and dangerous patience.

The Violet Guard handles treason, espionage, magical threats, assassination investigations, dangerous artifacts, major conspiracies, and threats to the Crown.

## Noble and Court Culture

Speech is careful in Crownward. Clothing is cleaner. Guards notice everything. Servants hear everything. Courtiers remember everything.

Deals may be made in gardens, galleries, hallways, court pews, or during walks around Portal Lake. A polite conversation may be a duel. A dinner invitation may be an accusation.

## Common Sights

- Violet-cloaked royal guards
- Watch officers in polished uniforms
- Noble carriages and floating chairs
- Petitioners with sealed documents
- Apprentices of the Fourfold Path
- House servants carrying coded flowers
- Priests walking toward Portal Lake
- Diplomats pretending not to spy

## Adventure Hooks

- A petitioner vanishes after being called into a private chamber.
- One of the gargoyles on the Concordant Spire wakes without orders.
- The Chamber of Charts loses a route map that should not exist.
- A noble duel is disguised as a garden party.
- Tobias Finchwillow asks for quiet help with a case he cannot give to the Watch.
- Garrick Thornwall recognizes a player character's name from a sealed file.
- A Lantern Walk memorial lantern relights itself after years of darkness.

## District Saying

> In Crownward, even silence has etiquette.
