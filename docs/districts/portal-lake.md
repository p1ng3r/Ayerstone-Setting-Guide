# Portal Lake

## The Heart of Ayerstone

Portal Lake is the vast central lake of Ayerstone, roughly two miles across. It dominates the center of the city and shapes nearly every district around it.

Above the lake hangs the **Waterplane Portal**: an immense glowing blue-white vortex connected to the Plane of Elemental Water. The portal floats 50 to 100 feet in the air near Noble Row, roughly a hundred feet from the southern ledge of the lake basin.

Water pours endlessly from the portal as a massive waterfall into the lake below.

## Appearance

The portal is visible as a suspended blue-white storm of light, foam, and impossible water. It roars constantly, though the sound changes with distance and wind.

During the day, mist rises from the lake and catches sunlight in bright arcs. At night, the portal glows like a cold star over the water, turning nearby roofs, towers, bridges, and terraces blue-white.

The lake is surrounded by noble terraces, waterworks, bridges, gardens, temples, overlooks, patrol walks, and carefully maintained civic stonework.

## Civic Importance

Portal Lake feeds:

- Canals
- Reservoirs
- Public fountains
- Waterhouses
- Cisterns
- River channels
- Bathhouses
- Irrigation systems
- Fire reserves
- Waterfall Ward
- Edge waterfalls

Without Portal Lake, Ayerstone would not function.

## The Waterplane Portal

The portal was opened by King Azour III. To most citizens, it is a civic miracle. To engineers, it is a permanent responsibility. To priests, it is a theological argument that never ends.

No one agrees whether the portal is fully stable.

No one wants to test the question.

## Lake Traffic

Small boats, waterkeepers, priest barges, noble pleasure craft, service boats, and Tidereach maintenance vessels cross the lake regularly.

Large vessels are not permitted near the portal's central fall zone.

## Sacred and Civic Spaces

Temples and shrines around the lake honor water, travel, safe return, drowned dead, civic founders, and the living power that keeps the city alive.

Noble Row uses the lake for ceremony and display. Waterfall Ward uses it for survival. Crownward uses it for symbolism. Tidereach uses it for everything.

## Festivals

### Lanterns on the Water

Citizens release floating lanterns onto the lake in remembrance of sailors, travelers, and loved ones who never returned.

### The First Pour

A civic festival honoring the opening of the Waterplane Portal. House Tidereach, House Aurelian, and temple groups all claim parts of the tradition.

## Dangers and Mysteries

- Strange shapes sometimes move beneath the water.
- Objects dropped into the lake occasionally return years later.
- Dreams near the lake can be unusually vivid.
- Water drawn during certain moonless nights refuses to freeze.
- Some priests claim the portal is not only pouring water into Ayerstone, but listening.

## Adventure Hooks

- A maintenance crew vanishes beneath the portal mist.
- A lantern released during a funeral floats upstream against the current.
- A noble's private dock reveals an old sealed water gate.
- Tidereach engineers discover a pressure change they cannot explain.
- A priest claims the lake has begun repeating names in the sound of the Falls.

## Local Sayings

> Stone builds a city. Water keeps it alive.

> Every secret in Noble Row eventually reaches the lake.

> The Falls never stop. Pray they never do.
