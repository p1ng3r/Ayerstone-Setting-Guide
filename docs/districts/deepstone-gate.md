# Deepstone Gate

## The Mine Mouth of Ayerstone

Deepstone Gate is the public face of House Ironmantle's mining empire. It sits near the Grand Docks and links surface Ayerstone to ore lifts, stoneworks, mineral offices, guarded shaftheads, heavy cranes, sealed passages, and deep infrastructure inside the world-shard.

If the Grand Docks are where Ayerstone breathes outward, Deepstone Gate is where it digs inward.

## Appearance

The district is heavy, vertical, loud, and practical.

Mine-head towers rise over deep works. Ore cranes move constantly. Levstone elevators hum beside older chain lifts. Stone roads are worn smooth by carts, boots, sledges, and heavy freight. Iron rails vanish into guarded doors. Dust settles on everything.

The whole district smells of stone, metal, lamp oil, sweat, wet earth, and old air.

## Location

Deepstone Gate sits close enough to the Grand Docks that ore, stone, crystals, aetherite-bearing material, salvage, chain components, and heavy machine parts can move between them without crossing half the city.

This proximity makes the district strategically important and politically sensitive.

## Major Features

### Ironmantle Liftworks

A massive complex of cranes, lifts, ore platforms, guarded work entrances, pulley towers, levstone freight rigs, and reinforced elevators descending into the island.

The Liftworks are controlled by House Ironmantle and watched carefully by guild inspectors, house guards, and contract clerks.

### Ore Courts

Sorting yards where stone, metal, crystal, and aetherite-bearing material are inspected, weighed, graded, marked, and assigned.

The Ore Courts are loud, dusty, and full of arguments about value.

### The Deep Registry

The office that tracks claims, work sites, mineral rights, sealed passages, accidents, collapses, inheritance disputes, and extraction permits.

A document from the Deep Registry can make a family rich, ruin a mine, or start a feud.

### Stone Chapels

Small shrines where miners pray before a work shift. Many are dedicated to stone spirits, ancestors, craft gods, dead miners, and local household saints.

### The Sealed Doors

Deepstone Gate contains old sealed entrances into parts of Ayerstone no longer used. Some are closed for safety. Some for ownership disputes. Some for reasons no one explains.

- Deep repairs
- Old passage sealing
- Mineral disputes

## House Ironmantle

House Ironmantle is strongest here. Its people know stone, weight, pressure, old supports, buried rooms, sealed shafts, and the dangerous truth that Ayerstone is not just a city but a body of stone with memory.

Ironmantle guards are not ornamental. They are there because what comes out of the deep is valuable, and sometimes what comes out is not supposed to.

## Culture

Deepstone Gate is practical and suspicious. People judge visitors by boots, hands, and whether they know when to get out of the way.

Workers respect silence before descent. Superstitions are common and rarely mocked. Whistling near shaftheads is considered bad luck in some crews. So is counting carts aloud.

- Things heard below but not seen
