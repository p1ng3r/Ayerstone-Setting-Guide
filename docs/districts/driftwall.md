# Driftwall

## The City Beneath the City

*Most people come to Ayerstone looking for something. Most of them arrive here first.*

Driftwall occupies much of the southeastern edge of the central island. It is pressed against retaining walls, cliff edges, old foundations, forgotten tunnels, and the industrial districts beyond.

Originally called the Drift because it collected sailors, immigrants, runaways, refugees, workers, adventurers, dreamers, and failures, the district became Driftwall as homes and shops were built against the great walls supporting the upper city.

## Appearance

Buildings are packed tightly together and built from wood, salvaged stone, ship timber, reused hull planks, and whatever else can be made to hold. Bridges connect rooftops. Laundry hangs between buildings. Colorful banners stretch over narrow streets.

To outsiders it looks chaotic. To locals it follows generations of hard-earned logic.

## The Gobble Inn

The Gobble Inn is owned and run by Bilk Cheffy, a goblin comfort-food master. Before opening the inn fifteen years ago, Bilk cooked for royalty, nobles, merchants, and dignitaries. Then he left that life behind.

Many first-time visitors walk in expecting the lowest tavern food in Ayerstone and leave stunned. Everything is cooked fresh daily because Bilk refuses to do it any other way.

Kaelyra Vosswick is the head waitress, accountant, and practical manager who keeps the Gobble Inn running.

## Shadow Brotherhood

The district is watched by the Shadow Brotherhood, a notorious criminal organization that grew out of the old Elven Brotherhood.

Three names define its history:

- Vaelis Moonshadow, the old elven guildmaster.
- Corvin Veyl, the human guild boss who is now dead.
- Sylas Thornveil, the current hidden master known as the Rat King.

Rumors say Sylas has found old tunnel networks beneath Driftwall and uses them to move unseen through Ayerstone.

## Local Saying

> You arrive through the Docks. You stay because of Driftwall.
