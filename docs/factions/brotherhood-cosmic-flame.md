# Brotherhood of the Cosmic Flame

## Dragon Empire of Fire and Chains

The **Brotherhood of the Cosmic Flame** is a dragon-led imperial power built by sovereign dragons, dragonfolk, scaled nobility, conquest fleets, arcane war-priests, and slave labor.

Outsiders sometimes mistake the Brotherhood for a mage order because its ships burn with arcane fire and its priests speak constantly of cosmic flame. That mistake rarely survives first contact.

The heart of the Brotherhood is not scholarship.

It is dragons.

It is dominion.

It is chains.

## Core Belief

The Brotherhood teaches that dragons were born to rule because dragons are the closest living expression of cosmic fire: power, will, appetite, radiance, and command.

Everything weaker exists to serve what is stronger.

In Brotherhood doctrine, enslavement is not an unfortunate tool of empire. It is one of the empire's central pillars. Conquered peoples are taken, sorted, branded, transported, and worked to expand the Flame.

To the Brotherhood, freedom is a privilege earned by bloodline, strength, service, or proximity to dragon power.

## Rule of the Dragon Pillars

The empire is ruled by great sovereign dragons and dragonfolk dynasties known collectively as the **Pillars of Flame** or simply the **Dragon Pillars**.

Each Pillar controls territories, fleets, tribute worlds, slave populations, military orders, and lesser client houses.

When a Pillar sends forth a fleet, worlds burn, routes close, and captains change course.

## Imperial Hierarchy

The Brotherhood's social order is rigid.

At the top stand the sovereign dragons.

Beneath them are dragonfolk nobles, flame-blooded houses, scaled officers, imperial priests, and arcane commanders.

Below them are favored scaled peoples, often including lizardfolk, kobolds, yuan-ti, and other reptilian or serpent-blooded groups who have been absorbed into imperial service.

At the bottom are conquered peoples, prisoners, debt-bound workers, and slaves.

## Arcane Power

The Brotherhood values magic, but magic serves empire.

Its magicians are trained as war assets, shipwrights, fire-callers, siege casters, slave binders, flame priests, and Arkengine specialists. Arcane power is respected when it strengthens dragon rule.

A brilliant mage who forgets their place may still become property.

## Ships and Fleets

Brotherhood vessels are designed to resemble disciplined dragons forged into warships.

Common features include:

- Dragon or serpent figureheads
- Segmented wing-like sails
- Scaled hull plating
- Dorsal flame spines
- Elevated command decks
- Red-and-gold banners
- Burning route lanterns
- Heavy boarding ramps
- Slave decks and chain holds
- Flame-marked Arkengines

Their fleets are feared for discipline, boarding actions, intimidation, and overwhelming displays of force.

## Symbol

The Brotherhood symbol is a red-and-gold flame within a celestial starburst, often marked with a serpent eye at its center.

The symbol represents dominion, dragon sight, cosmic flame, and imperial expansion.

## Relationship with Ayerstone

Ayerstone's Open Dock Compact allows Brotherhood ships to request harbor, trade, and repair under strict city law.

This creates constant tension.

The Brotherhood is one of the most hated factions among Ayerstone commoners, refugees, Freespacers, and former slaves. Brotherhood officers are watched closely by the Irystone Watch, the Violet Guard, private house agents, and half the dockside population.

Under Ayerstone law, no slave-taking, forced seizure, abduction, or foreign ownership claim may be enforced inside the city.

Brotherhood captains hate this.

Ayerstone does not care.

## Public Reputation

In Ayerstone, the Brotherhood is associated with:

- Dragon rule
- Slavery
- Red-and-gold warships
- Fire magic
- Harsh discipline
- Conquest fleets
- Dangerous diplomats
- Imperial arrogance

Many refugees in Driftwall and the Grand Docks fled Brotherhood raids or slave markets.

## Adventure Hooks

- A Brotherhood ship docks claiming diplomatic protection while secretly searching for escaped slaves.
- A kobold imperial clerk defects with route information.
- A Dragon Pillar sends an envoy to negotiate with House Starweaver.
- A Brotherhood slave brand begins glowing inside Ayerstone, violating harbor law.
- A red-and-gold warship arrives damaged and asks for repairs before its pursuers arrive.
- Refugees identify a Brotherhood officer as the butcher of their homeworld.
- The Violet Guard quietly hires adventurers to watch a Brotherhood delegation.

## Common Sayings

> Where the Flame lands, chains follow.

> A dragon smiles only when something is already burning.

> Never stand between a Brotherhood captain and what he thinks he owns.
