# Freespacers

## No Horizon Belongs to Kings

The **Freespacers** are not a nation, empire, kingdom, or republic.

They are a loose tradition of independent captains, frontier settlements, explorers, traders, salvagers, adventurers, colonists, and free ports who believe every person has the right to choose their own horizon.

Most Freespacers answer to no king.

Most Freespacer worlds govern themselves.

Their greatest gathering place is **Ayerstone**.

## Core Belief

Freespacers believe the stars were never meant to be owned.

No empire should dictate where honest people may travel. No sovereign should claim the Void itself. No captain should be forced beneath another's banner simply to survive.

Their philosophy is often summarized in three principles:

- **Freedom** - no captain rules another by birthright.
- **Responsibility** - freedom does not excuse cruelty.
- **Hospitality** - a harbor is sacred.

A Freespacer who murders, enslaves, or preys upon the helpless is no Freespacer at all, no matter what flag they fly.

## Becoming a Freespacer

Unlike the great empires and ordered factions of known space, there is no formal citizenship test for being a Freespacer.

No tax.

No bloodline.

No ancestry requirement.

No central registry.

If a captain, crew, settlement, or traveler declares themselves Freespacer and lives by the basic principles of freedom, responsibility, and hospitality, most Freespacers consider them part of the tradition.

This loose identity is both the movement's greatest strength and greatest weakness.

## Ayerstone and the Freespacer Tradition

Ayerstone is not the capital of the Freespacers.

The Freespacers have no capital.

But if the movement has a heart, it beats in Ayerstone.

Ayerstone's Open Dock Compact, independent harbor law, refugee protections, and refusal to bow to outside empires have made it the most famous Freespacer-aligned city in known space.

Every famous captain eventually visits.

Every refugee eventually hears its name.

Every faction respects or fears what happens when a free port refuses to kneel.

## The Freebooters

The Freespacers possess no standing navy in the imperial sense.

They do, however, have one organized defensive force: **the Freebooters**.

The Freebooters are a small but tightly knit volunteer navy of armed ships and sworn captains who protect Freespacer ports, escort vulnerable vessels, answer distress calls, fight pirates, and defend Ayerstone when needed.

They are not conquerors.

They are guardians of free harbors.

## Joining the Freebooters

Any qualified captain may apply to join the Freebooters at **Dock 44** in the Grand Docks of Ayerstone.

Applicants must:

- Own or command a void-capable ship.
- Possess legitimate combat capability.
- Swear the Freebooter Oath.
- Submit to vetting by the Dock 44 Captain's Board.
- Register their vessel under a Freebooter charter.

Once accepted, a captain receives:

- A registered Freebooter charter.
- Official ship registry.
- A blue-and-silver Freebooter flag or pennant.
- Access to distress networks.
- Legal recognition at Freespacer-aligned ports.
- Mutual aid agreements with other Freebooters.

Freebooter status is not granted lightly. A captain must have a ship capable of fighting, not merely fleeing.

## Dock 44

Dock 44 is one of the most famous berths in the Grand Docks.

It is not the largest dock, nor the cleanest, nor the most elegant. It is a working berth, battered by years of repairs, war stories, oath-taking, arguments, and emergency launches.

A weathered boardhouse beside the dock serves as registry office, captain's court, oath hall, message post, and unofficial tavern annex.

The wall behind the main desk is hung with old Freebooter flags from ships that never returned.

No one touches them without permission.

## The Freebooter Oath

> I claim no throne.
>
> I seek no chains.
>
> I will answer those who call.
>
> I will defend those who cannot.
>
> I will respect every honest captain.
>
> I will never raise my flag for tyranny.
>
> So long as I sail, another horizon remains.

## Flag and Symbol

The common Freebooter flag is blue and silver.

Its central symbol is a silver compass laid over an open eight-pointed star. The outer ring is intentionally incomplete to show that there is always another horizon.

Some captains add personal markings, ship badges, tally knots, or memorial stars, but the incomplete compass-star remains the identifying symbol.

## Relationship with Other Factions

### Brotherhood of the Cosmic Flame

Freespacers despise the Brotherhood's slavery and dragon imperialism. Freebooter ships have rescued escaped slaves and fought Brotherhood raiders more than once.

### Grelkin Cartel

Freespacers distrust Grelkin raiders but respect their independence. Some Grelkin captains trade honestly in Ayerstone. Others are hunted by Freebooters.

### Veilwardens

Freespacers see Veilwardens as useful but controlling. A Veilwarden inspection may save a ship, but no Freespacer likes being told what magic they are allowed to use.

### Underwake Syndicates

Freespacers and Underwake crews often know the same routes. Sometimes they trade. Sometimes they fight. Sometimes it is difficult to tell the difference.

### Council of the Void

Freespacers consider Council space one of the great forbidden frontiers. Most Freebooter captains refuse to enter it without overwhelming reason.

## Common Sayings

> No horizon belongs to kings.

> The stars are wide enough for everyone.

> Every star deserves a harbor.

> A free captain bows only to weather, hunger, and bad decisions.

> If a harbor closes to refugees, it is no harbor at all.

## Adventure Hooks

- A captain applies at Dock 44 with a ship that is clearly stolen.
- A Freebooter distress flag is found drifting in the Void with no ship attached.
- The Dock 44 Captain's Board asks the party to investigate a false Freebooter charter.
- A Brotherhood fugitive reaches Ayerstone and requests Freespacer protection.
- A Freebooter ship refuses orders because its captain believes the Board has been compromised.
- A registered Freebooter vessel vanishes near Council space.
- A young captain asks the party to help prove their ship is combat-ready before oath day.
