# Factions of Known Space

## Ayerstone and the Open Dock Compact

Ayerstone is not a province of any galactic empire, coalition, holy order, or trade league. It is an independent Freespacer-aligned city and one of the greatest neutral ports in known space.

Its survival depends on a simple civic truth:

> All may dock. None may rule.

This principle is formalized through the **Open Dock Compact**, the foundational harbor covenant of Ayerstone.

## The Open Dock Compact

*A covenant older than most current captains.*

> Any vessel may seek harbor. Any soul may seek refuge. Lay down your quarrel at our docks, obey the laws of Ayerstone, and no banner shall deny you sanctuary.

Under the Compact:

- Any vessel may request dock, aid, repair, trade, or refuge.
- Foreign wars may not be fought inside Ayerstone.
- Captains must lower hostile colors before entering port.
- Slaving, abduction, forced conscription, and unauthorized seizure of persons are forbidden within city law.
- Harbor law supersedes foreign law while within Ayerstone's jurisdiction.
- No outside faction may station troops, claim territory, or enforce foreign rule inside the city.
- Refugees may request sanctuary under the Crown and the harbor courts.

The Compact is one reason Ayerstone is wealthy, dangerous, and politically complicated. Diplomats, pirates, refugees, merchants, privateers, exiles, scholars, pilgrims, spies, and fugitives all pass through the same docks under the same law.

Common saying:

> Dock if you can pay. Pray if you can't. Rule us? Never.

## Major Factions

Known space is not ruled by one great authority. It is fractured between competing powers, philosophies, fleets, cults, empires, syndicates, and free captains.

### Freespacers

Independent captains, frontier worlds, private traders, smugglers, scouts, explorers, couriers, and free ports. Ayerstone is part of the Freespacer tradition and is often considered one of its spiritual capitals.

The Freespacers have no capital and no formal citizenship. To declare yourself Freespacer and live by its principles is usually enough.

Their organized volunteer navy is called the **Freebooters**, based at Dock 44 in the Grand Docks.

### Brotherhood of the Cosmic Flame

A dragon-led imperial power built by sovereign dragons and dragonfolk. It expands through conquest, fleet power, arcane might, and slavery. Its lower imperial classes often include lizardfolk, kobolds, yuan-ti, and other scaled peoples.

### Grelkin Cartel

A strict raider empire made up largely of orcs, goblins, hobgoblins, and kobolds. The Cartel is known for piracy, raids, stolen ships, and taking resources from weaker targets.

### Veilwardens

A militant containment order that does not seek to destroy magic, but to regulate, bind, monitor, and contain dangerous magical forces.

### Underwake Syndicates

Smugglers, black-market brokers, illegal route guides, thieves, fixers, engine-modders, and information dealers operating beneath official commerce.

### Faith of the Churn

A religious and philosophical movement teaching that some souls return through cycles of life until their purpose is fulfilled.

### Council of the Void

One of the great powers of known space, and possibly the least understood. The Council is alien, dark, mysterious, and feared. Those who enter Council territory rarely return, and those who do often come back changed.

### Starweaver Sky Lines

A powerful commercial route and passenger network tied to House Starweaver, the Celestial Trade and Shipping Consortium, and the Open Horizon Fleet.

## Ayerstone's Position

Ayerstone allows nearly every faction to dock, bargain, repair, worship, trade, and seek refuge under harbor law.

This does not mean all factions are trusted.

Brotherhood ships are watched for slavery and abduction. Grelkin crews are watched for theft and violence. Underwake agents are watched because they are Underwake agents. Veilwardens are watched because they watch everyone else. Council visitors are watched because no one knows what else to do.

Neutrality is not innocence.

It is a weapon Ayerstone uses to survive.
