# Council of the Void

## Beyond the Last Chart

Among the great powers of known space, none are more feared or less understood than the **Council of the Void**.

The Council is alien, dark, ancient, and almost entirely mysterious to outsiders. It does not behave like an empire, cartel, free port, kingdom, church, or trade league.

Its borders are poorly mapped.

Its fleets are rarely seen clearly.

Its people are profoundly alien.

Those who enter Council space rarely return.

Those who do often return changed.

## Public Knowledge

The average citizen of Ayerstone knows only a handful of things about the Council:

- It occupies a vast and poorly understood region beyond the mapped frontier.
- Its inhabitants are unquestionably alien.
- Trade is almost nonexistent.
- Diplomacy is rare and difficult.
- Expeditions into Council territory rarely come back.
- Returned crews often suffer memory loss, dreams, silence, or strange behavior.

Among Arkflight captains, one warning is nearly universal:

> Beyond the last chart, the Void begins to watch you back.

## Council Space

Council space is not simply dangerous because of pirates, monsters, or hostile patrols.

It is dangerous because it does not behave correctly.

Reports describe:

- Silent stars
- Fleets glimpsed only in reflections
- Cities that appear grown rather than built
- Ark Compasses turning inward
- Route markers changing shape
- Lifeveils pulsing without cause
- Communication spells returning unfamiliar voices
- Ships finding themselves where they already were

No two surviving reports agree completely.

That may be because the reports are wrong.

It may also be because Council space changes depending on who enters it.

## Appearance and Symbols

The Council has no widely recognized public heraldry.

Recovered symbols often involve black spirals, starless circles, pale geometric eyes, folded triangles, impossible knots, or arrangements of dots that cause headaches when stared at too long.

Some symbols vanish when copied.

Others appear later in places no one drew them.

## The Council Itself

No one knows whether the Council is:

- A government
- A civilization
- A collective mind
- A religious order
- A council of alien houses
- A machine intelligence
- A surviving First World power
- Something older than known species

The name **Council of the Void** may not be what they call themselves.

It may be only the safest translation available.

## Diplomacy

Diplomatic contact is rare.

When Council representatives appear, they often do so through intermediaries, projected forms, silent envoys, masked alien figures, or messages delivered through impossible means.

Some ambassadors claim the Council has no interest in conquest.

Others claim the Council already owns everything beyond the last horizon and simply has not explained this to younger peoples yet.

## Relationship with Ayerstone

Ayerstone allows almost any faction to dock under the Open Dock Compact, but Council vessels are so rare that most citizens have never seen one.

When a suspected Council craft enters port, the Grand Docks become quiet in a way no guard can order.

The Irystone Watch observes from a distance.

The Violet Guard watches closely.

The Fourfold Conclave sends scholars.

Freespacers pretend not to be nervous.

No one follows Council visitors unless paid very well or very foolish.

## Relationship with Freespacers

Freespacers consider Council space one of the greatest forbidden frontiers.

A few captains dream of charting it.

Most prefer to live.

Freebooters do not casually enter Council space. A registered Freebooter vessel that vanishes near Council borders becomes a matter of serious concern at Dock 44.

## Public Reputation

In Ayerstone, the Council is associated with:

- Alien silence
- Forbidden territory
- Missing ships
- Memory loss
- Dark stars
- Unreadable maps
- Untranslatable envoys
- Terrible curiosity

Children dare one another to whisper Council names into mirrors.

Adults tell them not to.

## Adventure Hooks

- A ship returns from Council space with no crew but every meal still warm.
- A Council envoy arrives at Ayerstone and requests one unnamed citizen.
- A Freebooter vessel vanishes after crossing a border no chart shows.
- A Starweaver map begins drawing routes into Council territory by itself.
- A Council artifact is sold in Merchant's Reach and everyone who touches it dreams of the same black city.
- A returned explorer cannot speak, but writes coordinates in their sleep.
- The Violet Guard hires adventurers to follow a Council visitor without being noticed.

## Common Sayings

> Beyond the last chart, the Void begins to watch you back.

> If Council stars are visible, you are already too close.

> Some borders are warnings, not lines.

> Very few return. Fewer return whole.
