# Cosmology of Arkflight

## The Oldest Stories

The oldest traditions of Arkflight agree on one point: before empires, before modern gods, before Ayerstone, and before mortal histories became reliable, the Void was not empty.

It was walked by impossible beings.

Modern scholars divide these beings into two great categories:

- **The Pillar Titans** - vast primal colossi of matter, force, and elemental law.
- **The Veiled Sovereigns** - stranger powers tied to thought, memory, hunger, love, grief, dreams, and other abstractions.

No one agrees whether these beings were gods, ancestors, cosmic accidents, ancient rulers, or living laws of reality.

Most faiths, universities, and void cultures preserve different fragments of the same unsettling idea:

> The worlds were shaped by powers that did not think like mortals.

## Public Knowledge and Forbidden Lore

Most ordinary people know the Titans and Sovereigns only through sailors' tales, temple carvings, scholars' arguments, and old route songs. Children hear stories of Stonefathers, Flame Titans, forgotten kings of memory, dream-sovereigns, and stars that should not exist.

Learned people know the subject is more dangerous.

Too many ancient ruins reference beings no modern church claims. Too many void phenomena seem connected to dead Titans. Too many cultures remember the same abstract Sovereigns under different names.

## Major Concepts

### The Pillar Titans

The Pillar Titans were primal beings of matter and force. They did not merely command stone, fire, ice, storm, or gravity. They embodied those principles so completely that worlds changed around them.

### The Veiled Sovereigns

The Veiled Sovereigns were powers of thought, spirit, identity, emotion, memory, hunger, dreams, balance, endings, and becoming. They did not need to break mountains to change history.

They could make a civilization forget why it built them.

### The First Worlds

The First Worlds are the oldest civilizations remembered in broken myth: worlds where mortals, Titans, Sovereigns, and raw magic may have touched the same reality.

### The Titan War

Some scholars believe the First Worlds did not fall because of outside invaders, but because the Pillar Titans fought each other.

### Titan Bones

Some void islands and asteroid fields may not be stone at all, but the bones of dead Titans.

### The Shadow Stars

Some stars emit not light, but absence. They are navigation hazards, religious omens, and objects of scholarly dread.
