# The Pillar Titans

## Moving Laws of Matter

Before Arkflight, before Ayerstone, before the empires of mortal kind, the Void was not empty.

It was walked by impossible beings.

The oldest traditions call one class of these beings the **Pillar Titans**.

The Pillar Titans were not gods in the priestly sense. They were not rulers in crowns or spirits waiting for prayer. They were raw elemental colossi: moving laws of matter, force, and creation.

A Pillar Titan did not cast stone magic.

It was stone given will.

A Flame Titan did not breathe fire.

It made fire remember it had once been alive.

## What They Represented

Ancient accounts associate Pillar Titans with primal principles such as:

- Stone
- Flame
- Storm
- Water
- Ice
- Gravity
- Metal
- Root
- Ash
- Starfire

These were not simply domains. They were identities.

A mountain might rise where a Stone Titan slept. Oceans might boil when a Flame Titan passed. Gravity might bend around the oldest of them. Forests might grow in the shape of a Root Titan's shadow.

Many ancient peoples believed planets were not made in the way modern scholars imagine.

They were shed, carved, wounded, seeded, or accidentally formed by the passage of Pillar Titans.

## Titans and the First Worlds

The oldest myths claim the First Worlds were built in an age when mortals, Titans, Sovereigns, and raw magic all touched the same reality.

Some early civilizations revered Pillar Titans as sacred anchors. Others studied them as natural laws. Some tried to bind them. Some tried to weaponize them.

This may have been the first great mistake.

To call upon a Titan was to place a hand upon a principle of reality and demand that it move.

Reality often moved.

It did not always move gently.

## Known Pillar Titans

The following names appear in surviving myths, ruin inscriptions, void sailor traditions, and disputed scholarly reconstructions.

### The Stonefather

The largest known Pillar Titan in common myth: a moving mountain range whose passing could raise continents or crush valleys into memory.

Some void scholars believe the Stonefather's remains may form entire asteroid belts.

Common symbol: a mountain with a single closed eye.

### Embermaw

A Titan of Flame said to have created volcanic worlds merely by passing nearby.

In some traditions Embermaw is a destroyer. In others it is the sacred source of forge-fire, transformation, and rebirth through burning.

Common symbol: a mouth full of suns.

### Deepmother

A Titan of Water associated with oceans, pressure, tides, and abyssal memory.

Some theologians claim the Plane of Water itself formed around her dreaming body. House Tidereach scholars generally refuse to endorse this theory in public, but they collect every text about her they can find.

Common symbol: a dark wave beneath seven droplets.

### The Iron Shepherd

A Titan of Metal associated with living metals, magnetic storms, ore veins, and impossible alloys.

Some rare metals found in the Void are said to be fragments of its flock.

Common symbol: a crook made of iron stars.

### The Last Storm

A Titan of Wind, pressure, lightning, and endless motion.

Sailors claim the Last Storm still circles the outer Void, never touching the same route twice. Some Arkflight captains swear that certain storms have intention.

Common symbol: a spiral cloud pierced by lightning.

### Root-That-Binds

A Titan of root, growth, world-seeding, and living structure.

Legends of massive void forests, living worldships, and plantlike moons are often connected to Root-That-Binds.

Common symbol: a root system wrapped around a star.

### Ashen Crown

A Titan of ash, aftermath, and what remains after burning.

Some cultures feared it as the final witness of destroyed worlds. Others honored it as the beginning of new soil.

Common symbol: a crown dissolving into ash.

### Starfire Wound

A Titan or Titan-fragment associated with starfire, celestial heat, radiant destruction, and wounds in the heavens.

Some believe Starfire Wound was never whole. Others believe it was injured before mortal history began.

Common symbol: a bleeding star.

## The Titan War

Many scholars believe the First Worlds did not fall because of some outside enemy.

They believe the Titans fought each other.

If true, the war was not a conflict of armies but of principles. Stone against storm. Fire against water. Gravity against root. Metal against starfire.

Worlds caught between them shattered, boiled, drowned, froze, or grew into shapes no living mind could endure.

No complete account survives.

That may be mercy.

## Titan Bones

Many void islands may not be asteroids.

They may be bones.

Entire trade routes pass through structures shaped like ribcages, skulls, vertebrae, claws, plates, and spines. Some are mineral. Some are fossil. Some are too large to be any natural creature known to modern scholars.

Sailors call them **Titan Bones** whether or not scholars approve.

Nobody knows what killed them.

## Modern Belief

Most people treat Pillar Titan stories as ancient myth.

Miners are less dismissive.

Navigators are less dismissive than miners.

Anyone who has sailed through a ribcage larger than a kingdom tends not to laugh at Titan stories again.

## Common Saying

> The Titans made the worlds. The Sovereigns taught them how to suffer.

Other sayings:

> Never build on stone that remembers walking.

> If the mountain has a heartbeat, leave.

> Old bones make good routes and bad dreams.
