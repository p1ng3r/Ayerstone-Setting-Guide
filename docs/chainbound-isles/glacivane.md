# Glacivane

## The Frozen Crown of the Chainbound Isles

Known to sailors as **Black Rime Isle**, Glacivane is one of the most remote and least explored of the Chainbound Isles.

The island is roughly 10 miles long and 13 miles wide at its broadest point. At its slimmest point it narrows to about 6 miles. Beneath the surface, however, Glacivane descends nearly 22 miles into the Void, tapering into a jagged frozen spear of stone and ice.

Unlike most of the Chainbound Isles, Glacivane knows no seasons. Snow falls year-round. Ice storms can emerge from clear skies without warning. Entire valleys vanish beneath fresh drifts overnight.

## Terrain

Glacivane is a frozen mixture of glaciers, snow forests, ice canyons, tundra, fir trees, alien plants, and mountains.

Near the center of the island rises a mountain called **the Crown**, standing roughly 1,369 feet high. It is taller than every other mountain on the island and can be seen from nearly anywhere on the surface.

## The Ruins at the Crown

At the base of the Crown lie the shattered remains of an alien civilization.

Tall crystalline spires rise from the snow, many cracked or collapsed. Strange statues stand among them: fur-lined, four-legged, two-armed beings preserved in ice. Some have eroded into abstract shapes. Others remain disturbingly intact.

Nearby are larger six-armed quadruped statues, many damaged by time, wind, and frost. Scholars argue whether these depict gods, war-forms, guardian constructs, or something else entirely.

The unknown people represented here are currently called **the Thulgrax** by researchers.

## The Black Rift

On the far side of the island, away from Ayerstone, lies an ice canyon filled with a deep black gelatinous substance called **Nightmelt**.

Nightmelt does not freeze. It swallows light, moves slowly without wind, and sometimes pulses as though something beneath it is breathing.

No one has determined whether it is mineral, organism, magical residue, or something stranger.

## Settlement

### Frostward Village

The only permanent settlement on Glacivane stands on the side of the island closest to Ayerstone.

The village has roughly 200 permanent residents, not counting adventurers, prospectors, guards, researchers, and other temporary visitors. Most buildings are carved from ice, reinforced with timber, stone, bone, and imported metal.

Frostward is struggling. The cold is constant, supplies are difficult, the ruins are dangerous, and no stable mining operation has yet succeeded.

The village contains:

- A research base with about 75 employees, including scholars, guards, cleaners, cooks, porters, and staff.
- A general store.
- A few expedition sheds.
- A small guard post.
- The Steamglass Hearth tavern and inn.

## The Steamglass Hearth

The Steamglass Hearth is the beating heart of Frostward.

From the outside it appears little more than a squat building carved from blue-white ice blocks reinforced with dark timber and whale-bone supports. Thick smoke rises from its chimneys day and night, and warm golden light spills through panes of enchanted frostglass.

Inside, however, lies the reason nearly every traveler remembers the place.

At the center of the tavern rests a naturally heated spring surrounded by smooth stone seating. Steam rises continuously into the rafters where it mingles with hanging lanterns and drying herbs. Patrons often sit waist-deep in the warm water while eating, drinking, negotiating contracts, or recovering from frostbite.

It is not uncommon to find a frost elf scholar discussing ancient ruins beside a scarred Arkflight captain while a pair of prospectors argue over mineral claims and a gnome researcher scribbles notes nearby.

The Hearth is jointly operated by several frost elf families and a close-knit clan of glacier gnomes. Guests quickly learn that no matter how rough the weather becomes outside, there is always hot food, warm drink, and a place by the spring waiting inside.

Local saying:

> If you die outside, the snow keeps you. If you live, the Hearth brings you back.

Many adventurers who set out toward the ruins return to the Steamglass Hearth carrying treasures.

Many more return carrying nightmares.

And some return carrying neither.

## Rule and Oversight

Glacivane is currently overseen by **Lord Berron Tidereach**, a noble of House Tidereach who convinced his house that the island may become one of Ayerstone's most important strategic assets.

His argument was simple: if the Waterplane Portal ever closed, Glacivane's glaciers could become a secondary emergency water source for the city.

He also believes the island's medicinal plants, alien flora, rare ores, and potential mineral deposits could one day make it self-sustaining.

## Lord Berron Tidereach

### Warden of Glacivane Waters

Lord Berron Tidereach arrived on Glacivane nearly four years ago after successfully convincing several members of House Tidereach that the frozen island represented Ayerstone's greatest untapped strategic asset.

He argued that if the Waterplane Portal ever failed, Glacivane's glaciers could become the kingdom's emergency water reserve. The proposal was initially mocked.

Then Berron volunteered to oversee the settlement personally.

Most expected him to return within a season.

Instead he stayed.

Berron is a broad, barrel-chested man with frost-red cheeks, a magnificent walrus mustache, and a booming laugh that can often be heard before he enters a room. He favors layered blue coats trimmed with white fur, heavy gloves, and polished boots crafted from tuskaner hide.

To outsiders he often appears larger than life.

To those who know him, he is larger than life entirely by choice.

No story involving Lord Berron ever seems to become smaller with age.

## The Hunt of Old Ice

Ask Berron about the enormous tuskaner skull mounted above his office hearth and he will gladly tell the story.

Usually twice.

According to Berron's preferred version, he ventured alone into the frozen interior armed with nothing but courage, determination, and his bare hands.

After three days of tracking, he confronted the beast atop a windswept ridge. The tuskaner charged. Berron met it head-on. The struggle lasted hours. Ice cracked. Mountains shook. The creature finally fell after receiving a mighty punch directly between its six eyes.

The audience often applauds.

The details vary slightly each telling.

The actual story is considerably less dramatic.

Berron carried a heavy hunting spear, two harpoons, three assistants, and enough supplies for a week-long expedition. Even then the hunt nearly killed everyone involved.

The tuskaner charged through two defensive positions, shattered a sled, injured one hunter, and nearly threw Berron from a cliff before finally succumbing to repeated harpoon strikes and a spear thrust through the neck.

Berron knows perfectly well that everyone knows the truth.

He simply enjoys the story more his way.

Despite his exaggerations, the hunt accomplished something important.

The successful kill proved that Glacivane could support permanent settlement.

Prior to that expedition many believed the island too dangerous to colonize.

Afterward, prospectors arrived.

Then scholars.

Then merchants.

Then adventurers.

Today the enormous skull remains mounted in Berron's office.

Visitors often notice deep gouges running along one side of the bone.

When asked about them, Berron always smiles.

Then he tells a different version of the story.

Each one somehow sounds even less believable than the last.

## Resources

No aetherite has been found on Glacivane.

Still, the island is valuable for:

- Medicinal alien plants
- Rare ores
- Strange ruins
- Potential emergency water reserves
- Ancient magical objects recovered from the ruins
- Research into Nightmelt

## Creatures of Glacivane

### Tuskaner

A massive mammoth-like creature with three trunks, six bulbous eyes on either side of its head, four huge tusks, and a long tail that gathers icicles.

### Glassmantle Skitters

Flat translucent insectoid creatures that glide beneath ice sheets and mimic reflections.

### Rime Choirs

Clusters of tiny floating frost-polyps that sing in strange harmonics. Their song can cause hallucinations and navigation errors.

### Hookjaw Lanterns

Blind seal-like predators with glowing throat sacs and hooked mandibles. They lure travelers through blizzards.

### Whiteback Grinners

Small six-limbed scavengers with too many teeth. Children fear them because they climb walls and laugh like coughing children.

### Moss-Eyed Snowstriders

Tall, gentle herbivores with mossy green eye clusters and branching leg joints. They are secretly much friendlier than their reputation.

### Shardwomb Eels

Long ice-burrowing creatures that swim through compacted snow and frozen soil, leaving glassy tunnels behind them.

## Apex Predator

### Six-Winged Glarewyrm

The most feared predator on Glacivane is the Six-Winged Glarewyrm.

It is said to be about 70 feet long, with six wings, six legs ending in vicious claws, three tails, and a single eye mounted on a long neck. The eye can fire jets of acid.

Glarewyrms viciously attack Arkflight ships. Worse, when one attacks, others sometimes follow.

The sheer winds around Glacivane also make Lifeveils difficult to maintain, so many pilots avoid flying too close.

## Frost Giant Rumors

Some adventurers claim that near the base of the Crown, close to a particular set of ruins, there is a tribe of frost giants.

No official survey has confirmed this.

No sensible researcher wants to volunteer.

## Mysteries

The greatest public mystery of Glacivane is what happened to the Thulgrax, the alien species seemingly represented by the statues at the ruins.

The most troubling private reports involve tremors rising from deep within the island's 22-mile frozen root.

Something far below the surface is moving.

## Why Adventurers Go

Glacivane is one of the least explored Chainbound Isles.

Few watch who comes and goes. The ruins are largely unmapped. The Nightmelt remains unexplained. Ancient magic items have already been recovered. The village is struggling and constantly needs help.

For adventurers willing to risk frostbite, monsters, ship attacks, lost ruins, and things buried under ancient ice, Glacivane is full of opportunity.

## Adventure Hooks

- A tremor cracks the ice beneath Frostward and reveals a tunnel leading downward.
- A researcher returns from the Crown speaking in a language no one knows.
- Nightmelt rises three feet overnight.
- A Six-Winged Glarewyrm attacks a supply ship and survivors need rescue.
- A Moss-Eyed Snowstrider leads travelers to a frozen statue that was not there yesterday.
- Lord Berron hires adventurers to recover a missing Tidereach survey team.
- The hot spring at the Steamglass Hearth turns black for exactly one minute.
- A Thulgrax statue encased in ice opens one eye.

## Local Sayings

> Glacivane does not thaw. It waits.

> The Hearth saves bodies. The snow keeps secrets.

> Never trust ice that hums.
